Understanding the Cash Flow Page
The Cash Flow page is the "Heartbeat" of your business. Here is what those three numbers mean:

A. Total Credit (Money In ↑)
This is the sum of every transaction where money entered your accounts.

Source: When a staff member pays back a "Receivable IOU."
Source: When you add money manually to a bank account (Deposit).
Source: When you eventually add "Bills/Invoices" and a client pays you.
Purpose: It tells you your total "Revenue" or "Inflow" for that period.

B. Total Debit (Money Out ↓)
This is the sum of every transaction where money left your accounts.

Source: When you create an "Expense."
Source: When you pay a supplier for a "Payable IOU."
Source: When you buy items via "Purchases."
Purpose: It tells you exactly how much you have spent.

C. Net Cash Flow (The Bottom Line)
Calculation: Total Credit - Total Debit = Net Cash Flow

If Positive (+): You are bringing in more money than you are spending. Your business is "Liquid."
If Negative (-): You are spending more than you are receiving. This is a warning sign that you might need to "Refill" your accounts from your main capital.
